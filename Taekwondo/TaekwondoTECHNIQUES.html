Taekwondo techniques are a dynamic blend of fast, high-level kicks, jumping/spinning kicks, and precise hand strikes and blocks designed for offense and defense, with a strong emphasis on speed, agility, and power. Key techniques include fundamental kicks (front, roundhouse, side), powerful spinning kicks, and strict stances.

Below are the most popular taekwondo techniques:

Name Description
Roundhouse Kick (Dollyo Chagi) Often the most used kick in sparring, targeting the side of the head or torso.
Front Kick (Ap Chagi) A quick, direct, and straightforward snap kick using the ball of the foot.
Side Kick (Yeop Chagi) A powerful, linear kick using the edge of the foot for breaking or pushing.
Back Kick (Dwit Chagi) A powerful counter-attack performed with a turning motion, ideal for stopping an opponent.
Axe Kick (Naeryo Chagi) A downward, chopping motion aimed at the head or shoulders.
Hook Kick (Huryeo Chagi) A whipping kick that uses the heel to strike, often following a missed roundhouse.
Spinning Hook Kick (Dweel Huryeo Chagi) A fast, spinning version of the hook kick used for knockouts.
Tornado Kick (Dolgae Chagi) A jumping, spinning roundhouse kick that adds momentum and speed.
Flying Side Kick (Twi Myo Yeop Chagi) An advanced, leaping kick frequently used in demonstrations and sparring.
Low Block (Arae Makgi) Defends the torso against low kicks or punches.
High Block (Eolgul Makgi) Protects the head from downward strikes.
Punch (Jireugi) A direct strike, usually aimed at the torso (Momtong).
Knife Hand Strike (Sonkal Taerigi) A strike using the outer edge of the hand.
